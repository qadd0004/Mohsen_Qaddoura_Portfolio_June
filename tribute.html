Nelson Mandela

A Rare Leader

Former President of South Africa

Here's a time line of Mr Nelson Mandelel :

  • 1918-Born Rolihlahla Mandela at Mvezo in the Transkei.
  • 1939-Enrols at the University College of Fort Hare, in Alice but get expelled in 1940.
  • 1941-Escapes an arranged marriage and start working at the law firm Witkin, Sidelsky & Eidelman.
  • 1942-Completes his BA through the University of South Africa.
  • 1944- Co-founds the ANC Youth League (ANCYL); marries Evelyn Ntoko Mase and they have four children together.
  • 1951-Elected president of the ANCYL.
  • 1952-Defiance Campaign begins; arrested and charged for violating the Suppression of Communism Act; sentenced to nine months imprisonment with hard labour, suspended for two years; Opens South Africa’s first black law firm with Oliver Tambo.
  • 1956-Arrested and later joins 155 others on trial for teason. All are acquitted by 29 March 1961.
  • 1960-Sharpeville Massacre; a state of emergency is imposed and he is among thousands detained; ANC is banned.
  • 1962-Sentenced to five years in prison for incitement and leaving the country without a passport.
  • 1963-Sent to Robben Island.
  • 1964-All except Rusty Bernstein are convicted and sentenced to life imprisonment.
  • 1982-Mandela is sent with others to Pollsmoor Prison.
  • 1985-Rejects South African President PW Botha's offer to release him if he renounces violence.
  • 1990-He is released, ANC ban is lifted, and get elected as ANC Deputy President.
  • 1993-Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize with president FW de Klerk.
  • 1994-Elected by the South African parliament as first president of a democratic South Africa.
  • 1996-Divorces Winnie Mandela.
  • 1999-Steps down after one term as president.
  • 2004-Announces that he will be stepping down from public life.
  • 2009-Attends the inauguration of President Jacob Zuma.
  • 2010-His second book Conversations with Myself is published.
  • 2011-His book Nelson Mandela By Himself: The Authorized Book of Quotations is published.
  • 2013-Dies at home in Johannesburg.

"What I will remember most about Mr. Mandela is that he was a man whose heart, soul, and spirit could not be contained or restrained by racial and economic injustices, metal bars, or the burden of hate and revenge. He taught us forgiveness on a grand scale."

Muhammad Ali
